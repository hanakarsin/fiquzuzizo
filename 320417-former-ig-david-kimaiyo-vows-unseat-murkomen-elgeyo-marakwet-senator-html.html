Former IG David Kimaiyo vows to unseat Murkomen as Elgeyo Marakwet Senator

July 2024 · 2 minute read

- Kimaiyo claimed the leader of Majority in the Senate was focused more on Tanga Tanga politics

- The former police IG said Murkomen began his first term well but deviated a long the way

- The ex-police officer said he understands challenges facing Elgeyo Marakwet residents and was ready to provide solutions

Former Inspector General of Police David Kimaiyo has vowed to send home Elgeyo Marakwet Senator Kipchumba Murkomen in the 2022 General Election.

Kimaiyo, who lost the same seat to Murkomen in the 2017 elections, said he shall ensure votes are better protected in the next polls after losing “unfairly” last time.

According to a report by Daily Nation on Tuesday, October 22, the former police IG claimed the leader of majority in the Senate has focused more on Tanga Tanga politics instead of articulating issues affecting the residents of Elgeyo Marakwet.

“God willing, I will vie again for the Senate seat in 2022. I am still ready to serve my people. I know their challenges and will be more than ready to provide solutions. This time round I will take it, just know that,” Kimaiyo asserted.

The former Kenyatta National Board chair is currently focusing on his real estate business and farming after his term ended three months ago.

Kimaiyo argued the senator started his first term well but deviated from “real” issues along the way.

“Murkomen is almost the age of my son. I think he is just 10 years older than my firstborn. He is among the youngest leaders we have. He started well,” he said.“During his first term he focused on issues affecting residents. But he has deviated from the role the people of Elgeyo-Marakwet gave him," claimed the ex-police officer.

In a quick rejoinder, Murkomen clapped back at the former police IG through his Twitter page on Tuesday, October 22 and reffered him to a name used in local comedy; Plesident Kingston.

In the 2017 General Election, Kimaiyo came second against Murkomen after garnering 21,884 votes against his competitor’s 117,398 votes.
