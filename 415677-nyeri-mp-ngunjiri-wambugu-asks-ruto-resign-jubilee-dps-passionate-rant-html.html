Nyeri Town MP Ngunjiri Wambugu Asks Ruto to Resign from Jubilee after DP's Passionate Rant

August 2024 · 3 minute read
  • On Tuesday, June 15, Deputy President William Ruto took to Twitter to rant after President Uhuru Kenyatta indicated he will back a NASA presidential candidate
  • The embattled politician, however, said that with the support of God he would soldier on with his race to state house
  • Nyeri MP Ngunjiri Wambugu said the ruling Jubilee Party was better off without the DP

Nyeri Town member of parliament Ngunjiri Wambugu has asked Deputy President William Ruto to quit the ruling Jubilee Party after his passionate rant on Tuesday, June 15.

In a series of Facebook posts, the vocal Jubilee supporter argued the ruling party was stronger than ever before after the DP left albeit unofficially.

"Ruto should be told that Jubilee - like the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM)- will not die because he left. Someone tell Ruto that his leaving Jubilee doesn’t mean it stopped existing. He also left ODM and it’s also very much alive. In fact, both parties are doing better since he left," Wambugu opined.

The first-term lawmaker, a fierce defender of the Jubilee administration, asked Ruto to resign from the outfit and build his United Democratic Alliance which he has openly embraced.

"He keeps asking people to follow the constitution. Shouldn't he do the same and resign from Jubilee and defect to UDA? Even God doesn’t like people who are neither hot nor cold!" the MP insisted.

Ruto's rant

Wambugu's remarks came after Ruto sarcastically welcomed President Uhuru Kenyatta's plan to back a National Super Alliance (NASA) presidential candidate in the 2022 General Election.

While responding to a headline in one of the local dailies that his boss will openly support one of the principals of the defunct alliance, the DP wondered what would happen to the over eight million Kenyans who sang tano tena.

The embattled politician, however, said that with the support of God he would soldier on with his race to state house.

"Eucho! Ngai fafa mwathani!! So, what happens to the Thurakus, the kumìrà kùmèrà contingent, the eight million of us?? None, no youth, no woman, no man of the eight million who woke up early and voted three times for UK/WsR ticket merit support? Sawa tu! Tutajipanga na support ya mungu (We will strategise with God's support)."

Wambugu in his response to the tantrums said Ruto should not complain since he had been fielding candidates against Jubilee where he still serves as the deputy party leader.

"So this guy has been fielding candidates against the president’s (and his own) party just to undermine Uhuru, but is now acting all surprised when a paper says Uhuru might support someone else rather than him? " Wambugu quipped.

Ruto's plan B

In March 2021, Ruto revealed he had a different plan if he would be ousted as the Jubilee deputy party leader.

The veteran politician hinted he would use UDA as his political vehicle for the 2022 General Election.

“If it gets to a point where they do not want us to be there in Jubilee, then we must have a different plan. You cannot wait to hit a wall. From now on, if there is no agreement, then we will plan ourselves with UDA. We have built UDA in partnership with Jubilee. It is not us starting afresh. We are just going on with what we already have,” Ruto said.
