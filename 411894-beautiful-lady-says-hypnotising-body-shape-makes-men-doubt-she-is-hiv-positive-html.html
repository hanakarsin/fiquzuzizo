Beautiful Lady Says Hypnotising Body Shape Makes Men Doubt She Is HIV Positive

April 2024 · 3 minute read

- Susan Metta warned Kenyans against determining a person's HIV status at face value

- The beauty believes she was infected with the virus in 2016 but is unaware of who infected her

- Surprisingly, Susan was relieved when she tested positive for HIV as she had imagined worse

A 40 year old woman living with HIV has urged Kenyans not to determine a person's status by simply looking at them.

Susan Metta said many Kenyans find it hard believing she has the virus. Speaking to BBC, Susan advised Kenyans to do their due diligence before getting intimate with a partner.

“Stop determining someone's HIV status by simply looking at them. Smooth skin and a well-built body do not mean one does not have the virus,” she said.

Falling sick

Narrating her story to Anne Ngugi, Susan recounted how she was diagnosed with the virus.

According to the beauty, she had a well-paying job managing restaurants in South Sudan from 2008 to 2018.

In 2018, she started having random symptoms but attributed them to the blistering heat.

“I would suddenly feel hot or cold. I thought it was my body responding to the weather,” she said.

In September 2018, Susan’s employment contract was terminated without prior notice. She fell into depression and started heavily consuming alcohol.

“Soon after arriving back in Kenya, I developed a cough which did not ebb despite taking medication. I started rapidly losing weight, and my hair changed colour,” she narrated.

Sores on the upper lip prompted her to go to the hospital.

On January 14, 2019, she decided to undertake a test, but her courage failed, and she returned home. She took the test two days later after her two sisters accompanied her.

Happy with results

Surprisingly, Susan was relieved when she tested positive for HIV.

“I finally took the test on January 16, 2019, after ailing for almost six months. When the results came, I smiled and told the medical practitioner to give me ARVs. I was happy because I feared I had cancer,” she said.

Susan suspects she contracted HIV in 2016 even though she is unsure who infected her as she had multiple partners.

The beauty has a YouTube channel which she uses to educate society on HIV and highlight the challenges persons living with the virus face.

"At the end of the day, I cannot be your keeper. You do not know me. I am not related to you, yet you want to bed me without protection,” she said.
