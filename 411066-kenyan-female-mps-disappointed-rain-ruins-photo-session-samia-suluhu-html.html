Kenyan Female MPs Disappointed after Rain Ruins Photo Session with President Samia Suluhu

August 2024 · 2 minute read

- The female legislators could not help miss the once in a while chance that had just presented itself

- They were, however, disrupted by rains which they could not withstand, their urge to pose to Samia notwithstanding

- The visiting head of state completed her two-day visit, that coming after her address to joint parliament seating

Sudden downpour in Nairobi snatched female legislators the glory of posing for snaps with visiting Tanzania's president Samia Suluhu.

Samia was on a two-day state visit to Kenya, which was climaxed by her address to a joint parliament seating convened at the National Assembly buildings in Nairobi.

The female leaders had camped outside the buildings, singing as they eagerly waited to engage Samia in a momentous photo session.

However, the wait was disrupted by torrents that saw them scamper away to seek shelter.

Samia, who landed in Kenya on Tuesday, May 4, had a busy day on her last day of the visit.

Earlier on Wednesday, the Tanzanian leader spoke to a business forum that united corporate captains from Tanzania and Kenya, a function that was also graced by her host, President Uhuru Kenyatta.

During the forum held at the Nairobi hotel, the two leaders announced to have reached various pacts that would see the thriving of businesses in the two countries.

In his address, Uhuru announced a raft of incentives aimed at wooing Tanzanian investors to Kenya, including lifting work permits and visa requirements.

He said his administration would do all in its power to eliminate all non-tariff barriers for Tanzanian investors coming to do business in Kenya.

On her part, President Suluhu outlined the reforms being undertaken by her administration to enhance ease of doing business and welcomed Kenyan businesses to invest in Tanzania.
