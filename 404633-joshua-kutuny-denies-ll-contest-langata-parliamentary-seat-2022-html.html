Cherangany MP Joshua Kutuny has dismissed speculation that he will be vying for Lang'ata parliamentary seat in 2022.

Kutuny said he was focused on delivering for the people of Cherangany and harboured no dream of contesting for a parliamentary seat in Nairobi.

Speaking to Citizen TV on Tuesday, March 2, night, the vocal lawmaker asked his constituents and concerned Kenyans to treat the reports as false and misleading.

"I am serving the people of Cherangany and I love Cherangany so no...I am not interested in Lang'ata," said Kutuny.

Raila's hand in Langata politics

Rumours have been rife in recent months that ODM leader Raila Odinga was planning to field the youthful Jubilee lawmaker in Lang'ata to battle it out with the current MP, Nixon Korir.

Korir contested for the seat on the Jubilee Party ticket in 2017 and trounced the then ODM lawmaker Joash Olum.

Before Korir's victory in 2017, Lang'ata had been under Raila's fold for more than 20 years and was one of the constituencies perceived to be his stronghold.

He served as the MP for the city-based constituency (which included Kibra) from 1992 to 2012.

In 2013, the new constitution barred him from contesting as an MP since he was a presidential candidate.

He endorsed Olum, who beat Korir with a margin of over 7 000 votes. However, In 2017, Korir returned to the ring and floored the ODM lawmaker.

Plan to recapture Lang'ata

Raila is said to be keen to recapture the seat from Korir, who is an ally of Deputy President William Ruto.

It is against this backdrop that speculation emerged linking Kutuny to Raila's plot to fend off Ruto's influence in the city constituency.

Although in Jubilee, Kutuny is a close ally of Raila and has severally shown his great admiration for the ODM leader's style of leadership and politicking.

In 2007, the vibrant lawmaker was elected Cherangany MP on ODM ticket and has since maintained a cordial relationship with its leader.

President's ally

The Jubilee lawmaker has also endeared himself to President Uhuru Kenyatta and is one of the trusted young leaders in the Jubilee Party.

In 2013 when he lost his parliamentary seat, Uhuru appointed him as one of his political advisors domiciled at state house.

On Monday, March 2, Kutuny was appointed the ruling party's deputy secretary-general after Soy MP Caleb Kositany was kicked out over disloyalty and gross misconduct.

Kositany is an ally of Ruto and one of the active members of the Tanga Tanga group, which is now operating under the newly formed United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party.
