Kenyans Raise Concerns after William Ruto Pulls Relatively Small Crowd in Murang'a

July 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Deputy President Wiliam Ruto graced an event in Murang'a county where a local TV station was being launched
  • He later traversed Kenol town where he met and addressed residents
  • A section of Kenyans, however, noticed the crowd that received him was relatively smaller as compared to the ones in previous rallies

On Saturday, October 2, Deputy President William Ruto was briefly in Murang’a County, where he joined the local leaders during the launch of a local television station dubbed Spirit TV.

He later made his way to Kenol town where he was acknowledging the residents gathered to hear from him.

As usual, Ruto shared a few pictures taken during the visit.

However, a section of Kenyans noticed the cold reception accorded to him signified by the diminutive crowds he met during the stopovers he made earlier in the day during his visit.

This was contrary to the previous rallies in the locality which are known to have pulled large crowds.

The concerned Kenyans attributed the decline to the recent visit by the ODM party leader Raila Odinga who had been accompanied by top-tier politicians from the Mt Kenya region.

Others claimed mobilisation had not been undertaken suggesting the deputy president had been ferrying crowds to the areas he visit.
