Thirdway Alliance has pulled out of the 2022 presidential race, saying they will field candidates for other seats.

Partnership with other parties

In a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke, the party stated that the move had been necessitated by its bid to partner with other political parties.

The party further divulged that they will field candidates in other elective seats across the country.

“Thirdway Alliance Kenya will not be fielding a presidential candidate in 2022; however, we will be fielding candidates in all the other elective positions,” the statement read.

At the time, the party welcomed aspirants seeking to join the party; however, they must meet the minimum qualification as stipulated in the elections act.

“The call for aspirants on our party ticket is already out there. Our doors are open to all, and our party tickets are available based on qualification, moral and social integrity, as well as fair representation,” they added.

Need to change tactics

The party's Secretary-General Fredrick Okango noted that there is a need for the party to change its tactics for the party to experience good results.

GS said that the party will be championing good leadership through initiatives such as Punguza Mzigo Kenya, which seeks to unite and heal the country.

“It is our deepest conviction that we cannot do the same things the same way we do every election cycle, and if we do, then we will be foolish to expect a different result,” Okango said.

The SG also noted that the party is looking forward to fielding and supporting candidates who are of integrity to enhance good governance and proper management of the country’s resources.

This comes few days after Okango insisted Ekuru Aukot was expelled by the party and can no longer transact the outfit's business.

Okango said the party closed Aukot's case when he refused to appear before its disciplinary committee in August 2020 to respond to charges of corruption and gross misconduct levelled against him.
