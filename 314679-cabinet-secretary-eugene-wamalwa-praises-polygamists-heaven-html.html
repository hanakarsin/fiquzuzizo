Cabinet Secretary Eugene Wamalwa praises polygamists, says they will go to heaven

May 2024 · 3 minute read

- The Cabinet secretary quoted references from the Bible of men who were close to God but were polygamous

- According to the CS, biblical men like Abraham, Jacob, David and Solomon had many wives but still went to heaven

- Wamalwa argued polygamist were allowed in heaven as long as they make peace with their God and acknowledge Him as their creator

- However, in 2018, Anglican Archbishop Ole Sapit dismissed claims that polygamy was among biblical teachings

When Kiambu Woman Rep Gathoni wa Muchomba asked able men from her Kikuyu community to marry many wives in 2018, she may have just said what many people are afraid of saying.

Muchomba said polygamy will offer solution to alcoholism and street families, saying the upbringing of children in the absence of their fathers was the major cause of such vices.

Well, joining the debate is Devolution Cabinet Secretary Eugene Wamalwa who, while speaking at a funeral, argued that polygamists will also go to heaven, NTV reported.

While quoting biblical references, Wamalwa said a number of men from the holy writ who were very close to God and are known to have gone to heaven, were all polygamous.

"Today I have received a book titled Polygamist Go to Heaven, look at God's friends starting with Abraham, he did not have one wife, then Jacob and King David then Solomon is the one who broke the record," he said.

According to the CS, he bought the idea from an elderly theologian who had authored the book, Polygamist Go to Heaven Too.

"Today I want to agree with the old man that polygamists go to heaven as long as you have made peace with your God and acknowledge him as your maker," he added.

This, however, contradicts what Anglican Archbishop Jackson Ole Sapit said in 2018 noting the church will oppose any laws meant to legalise polygamy as it was not part of Bible teachings.

“The church is very clear through the teachings of the Bible especially the New Testament that one man and one woman make a family," he said."We want to make it clear from the perspective that as a church, a family is one wife and one husband joined together to form a couple and form a family,” added Ole Sapit.

This was after Wamuchomba, a wife and a mother, said it was hypocritical and pretentious to keep quite on the subject, which drew mixed reactions, saying polygamy is a culture well entrenched in Africa.
