William Ruto Postpones all Public Rallies in Adherence to COVID-19 Protocols

April 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Deputy President William Ruto has postponed all his scheduled public functions until further notice
  • The deputy president said the decision was informed by the new guidelines issued by the Ministry of Health to curb the spread of COVID-19
  • The move by the DP came shortly after Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe announced a 30-day ban on all public gatherings
  • The CS also banned office meetings adding that several government meetings have been postponed

Deputy President Wiliam Ruto has cancelled all his scheduled public rallies following a ban on gatherings placed by the Ministry of Health to curb the spread of COVID-19.

Ruto made the announcement adding that the public engagements had been cancelled until further notice because the COVID-19 situation in the country had escalated.

Making the announcement on his social media pages, the second in command further urged Kenyans to take individual responsibility and adhere to guidelines issued.

"The COVID-19 situation in the country has escalated as stated by GoK this afternoon. My scheduled public engagements are hereby postponed until further notice," Ruto tweeted. "We all should take individual responsibility and adhere to guidelines issued so as to control this ravaging pandemic," he added.

The DP's move comes after Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe ordered a 30-day ban on all public gatherings and the postponement of other government meetings.

In addition, Kagwe directed that all forms of physical/congregational worship be permitted in the country under the guidance of the Inter-Faith Council protocols.

However, the in-person worship is still limited to a third of the venue's capacity and strict adherence to the Ministry of Health's guidelines and protocols.

Employers in the public and private sectors and government offices, organizations, and corporations have been urged to allow employees to work from home.

At the same time, the health minister noted that there had been a sharp increase of COVID-19 cases in Kiambu, Kajiado, Lamu, Makueni, Muranga, Taita Taveta and Tana River County.

Meanwhile, the country has continued to record many COVID-19 cases as 945 people tested positive for the virus on Friday, July 30, from a sample size of 7,295 tested within 24 hours.

The positivity rate is now 13.0%. Of the cases, 910 are Kenyans, while 35 are foreigners. In addition, 481 are males, while 464 are females.

The youngest was an eleven-month-old infant, while the oldest was a 100-year-old. Total confirmed positive cases are now 201,954 and cumulative tests so far conducted are 2,124,274.
