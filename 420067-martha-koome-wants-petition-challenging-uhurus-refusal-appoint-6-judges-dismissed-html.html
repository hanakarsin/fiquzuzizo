Martha Koome Wants Petition Challenging Uhuru's Refusal to Appoint 6 Judges Dismissed

April 2024 · 3 minute read
  • Chief Justice Martha Koome said the petition filed by Katiba Institute was bad in law and was an abuse of the court process
  • Katiba Institute's petition was challenging President Uhuru Kenyatta's decision to drop the six judges initially recommended by the Judicial Service Commission (JSC)
  • Uhuru had said his decision was on the integrity issues around the said judges

In what seems to be a shift from the initial hardline stand, Chief Justice Martha Kooome, alongside the Judicial Service Commission (JSC), has asked the High Court to throw out the petition filed by the Katiba Institute regarding the rejection of six judges initially recommended by the JSC.

The lobby group had sought to have President Uhuru Kenyatta impelled to have the six judges sworn in alongside their colleagues.

Koome had been among the quarters that questioned Uhuru's decision to cherry-pick the 40 judges, 34 of whom took their oaths in June.

"As we welcome our seven new justices at the court of appeal, this is a bitter-sweet moment for the four colleagues not with us. I reiterate my position as chief justice and chair of JSC to state that all persons recommended by JSC must be appointed as judges," she had insisted.

She now says the Katiba Institute petition is bad in law and which contravenes the process of the court.

The case was slated for a hearing today by a three-judge bench.

The group held that Uhuru had no powers to decided who is capable and who is not of becoming a judge.

Koome, through her lawyer, said she could not revisit the decision behind the appointment of the judges citing the expiry of her mandate and that of the JSC in the case.

On his part, through his lawyer Waweru Gatonye, Uhuru asked the court to adjourn the case, stating that the awaited decision of the Court of Appeal would uphold his decision to drop the six judges.

Uhuru stays put

While presiding over the swearing-in of the 34 judges last month, Uhuru had said his decision to refuse the nomination of Justice George Odunga, and five others was purely based on integrity.

"Just like you today, I too took an oath, it is not open to me to turn a blind eye to reports of our state organs. As long as I serve as president, I will choose right over the convenient, I will choose the hard over the easy and I am not doing this for myself but for the people of Kenya and posterity," he stated in June during the swearing ceremony held at State House, Nairobi.

Other judges who failed to make it to the Court of Appeal were Weldon Korir and Aggrey Muchelule.

Magistrates Cheruiyot Judith Omange and Evans Makori, on the other hand, were dropped from the High Court list.
