1. Istana Nurul Iman Palace, Brunei (2.15 million square feet)

Who has the biggest house in the world? The huge luxurious palace - Istana Nurul Iman Palace, is undoubtedly the world's biggest home. It is the official residence of the Sultan of Brunei, Hassanal Bolkiah. It occupies a space of 2.15 million square feet.

The house was designed in 1984 by Leandro V. Locsin and constructed by the Filipino firm - Ayala International. It impresses most people, and they refer to it as the Palace of Faith Light. It has no less than 1,788 rooms and 257 bathrooms, a 110 car garage, horse stables, banquet hall, swimming pools, and even a mosque that can gather 1,500 people.

How much is the biggest house in the world? The cost of Istana Nurul Iman Palace is estimated at a whopping $1.4 billion.

2. Antilia, in Mumbai, India (400,000 square feet)

An Indian pharmaceutical magnate called Mukesh Ambani is the owner of Antilla. It is a 27 story 550-foot tall building in the heart of Mumbai, India. The skyscraper has a unique architecture, with each floor designed differently and using different materials.

The whole building was constructed with Vastu Shastra as the central philosophy in mind. Vastu Shastra is a traditional Hindu art of building houses. Every floor has its purpose, from car garages to spas, gyms and indoor pools, and even a helipad on top.

The house occupies 400,000 square feet, and its construction cost $1 billion. However, this is probably a drop in the ocean for the owner, considering that he is the wealthiest Indian and is ranked eighth globally.

3. Biltmore Estate, Asheville, North Carolina, USA (178,926 square feet)

George Vanderbilt - the grandson of shipping and railroad magnate Cornelius Vanderbilt, built the house in 1895. The house rests on 178,926 square feet of space and features a French Renaissance chateau-style architecture. It is the largest privately-owned home in the United States.

5. Witanhurst, London, England (90,000 square feet)

Witanhurst is London’s largest private home. It was built between 1913 and 1920 on a 90,000 square feet plot in Highgate - a wealthy hilltop neighbourhood north of the city centre. Arthur Crosfield, an English soap magnate, first owned the mansion.

It was designed in the Queen Anne style and contains twenty-five bedrooms, a seventy-foot-long ballroom, and a glass dome. Over Hampstead Heath and across the capital, the views from its gardens were among London's loveliest. The mansion is reportedly owned by Russian billionaire Andrey Guryev, who bought it in 2008 for nearly $64 million.

Modern Versailles is situated in Orange County, Florida. The mega-mansion belongs to Jaqueline and David Siegel - the CEO of Westgate Resorts. The huge mansion was so long under construction that at some point, the couple came close to losing their spacious apartment.

7. La Reverie, Palm Beach, Florida (84,626 square feet)

What celebrity has the biggest house? It is, without a doubt, Sydell Miller - co-founder of hair care company Matrix Essentials. She is the owner of La Reverie. The home has ten bedrooms, nineteen bathrooms, and a mix of hardwood and marble floors.

There are tennis courts, an outdoor pool, and an ornate fountain at the house's front. It sits on land that stretches from the Atlantic Ocean to Intracoastal Waterway. At the back of the mansion, a manicured lawn leads to an oceanfront beach that's 350 feet long.

In 2019, it became the biggest house in the world for sale. Hedge fund manager Steven Schonfeld was said to be buying the Palm Beach mega-mansion at a value between $100 -$200 million. The estate wasn't listed on the open market, so until the sale closes and the deed hits the registry, there's no public record.

8. Villa Leopolda, The French Riviera (80,000 square feet)

The mansion was designed by American architect Ogden Codman, Jr. This gorgeous home on the French Riviera was built in 1931 for King Leopold II of Belgium as a gift for his mistress. It is on 80,000 square feet space and has nineteen bedroom suites, numerous dining rooms, and kitchens.

Additionally, the home has a private theatre across 7432 square metres of built-up space and twelve pools, sprawling gardens, an outdoor kitchen, a commercial greenhouse, and a helipad.

9. The One, Bel-Air, California (74,000 square feet)

Apart from being one of the largest, it is also the most expensive home globally. It occupies 74,000 square feet and comprises twenty bedrooms, thirty bathrooms, and a specially designed jellyfish room. The price of this modern beauty rises to the astounding sum of $500 million.

It belongs to Nile Niami, who bought it in late 2012 for $28 million from Rita Kogan, the late video game heiress, and daughter of Space Invaders creator Michael Kogan. It's difficult to grasp this mansion's sheer size by just looking at the pictures or numbers, although it's massive.
