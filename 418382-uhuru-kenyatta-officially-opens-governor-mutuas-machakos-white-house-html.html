Machakos governor Alfred Mutua has officially opened the Machakos International Convention Centre and governor's "White House" office.

The grand opening ceremony was witnessed by President Uhuru Kenyatta, ODM leader Raila Odinga and the Wiper party boss Kalonzo Musyoka on Friday, July 9.

"I wish to thank all the leaders who graced the official openings of the ultra-modern (and money-making) Machakos International Convention Centre (located at the iconic People’s Park) & the Machakos Governor’s office(“White House”) by President Uhuru Kenyatta. Ahsante sana Rais U.K," he posted.

Dubbed the "White House" because of its resemblance to the US White House, the magnificent office is said to have cost the county KSh 350 million and was constructed within nine months.

Sitting on a spacious piece of land in Masaku, the county office presents a sight to behold from afar, and one could easily mistake it for the president's State House office in Nairobi.

However, Mutua is said to have drawn the inspiration to build the state-of-the-art office from the United States.

The office has two wings, the West Wing which would be hosting the governor, his chief of staff and executive meeting lounges, and the East Wing which would house the Office of the First Lady, the governor’s advisers and other support staff.
