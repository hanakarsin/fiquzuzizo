Comedian Njugush Remembers Walking Barefoot in Primary School in Bid to Fit In

June 2024 · 3 minute read

- Njugush has recounted the challenges that came along with being the son of a cleric

- As a child, they were forced to often relocate due to his father's work, and he recalled an experience at Mau Narok Primary School

- According to the comedian, none of the pupils had shoes, and he walked barefoot to fit in

Entertainer Timothy Kimani alias Njugush has recounted the challenges he faced as a child switching schools due to his dad’s work.

The comedian said his father is a cleric and would often be transferred to new workstations, and the family relocated with him.

According to Njugush, the constant movement exposed him to starkly different environments.

Narrating one of the experiences, the father of one recounted how he was forced to attend school barefoot in a bid to fit in.

“There is a school I attended in Nakuru county where the kids never wore shoes. Wearing shoes would make you stand out, so I would put shoes in my bag since my fellow learners would ask to try them on,” Njugush said.

On the other hand, Njugush spoke of experiencing cultural shock after they moved to Eldoret town.

“In Eldoret, I was surprised to find we had a fridge in the house. I would close and open the fridge trying to figure out when the light goes off,” he said.

Njugush revealed that the constant movement made them a close-knit family.

The comedian also spoke of the challenges he faced after joining a boarding secondary school.

“I had a hard time adjusting. I lost several shirts. My mattress and other items were also stolen,” he narrated.

''You know it is a huge responsibility when you call her 'his wife'. I don't have a wife I have a 'dem' (girlfriend),'' Njugush said.
