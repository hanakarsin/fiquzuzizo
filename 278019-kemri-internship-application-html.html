KEMRI internship application: vacancies and procedure

April 2024 · 5 minute read

The Kenya Medical Research Institute (KEMRI) stands as a beacon of scientific advancement and healthcare innovation in East Africa. Established in 1979, KEMRI has been at the forefront of medical research, tackling some of the most pressing health challenges facing the region. For those eager to immerse themselves in this dynamic environment, the KEMRI internship program offers a gateway to invaluable experience and learning opportunities. Here is all you need to know about the KEMRI internship application for 2024.

TABLE OF CONTENTS

KEMRI, as an employer, creates KEMRI jobs targeting scientific individuals with expertise in various sciences and support staff who help in the institute's day-to-day activities. This creates an objective and effective working environment. The institute also provides internship vacancies in Nairobi and other attachment opportunities in Kenya for students pursuing diplomas and degrees in specified sciences that interest the institute.

What is KEMRI internship?

The KEMRI internship is a programme offered by the Kenya Medical Research Institute to expose young graduates in this field to the practical/technical aspects of research.

The research internship provides an opportunity for young graduates to gain knowledge and experience in the ever-changing research space under the tutelage of senior scientists.

In most cases, interns serve under scientific staff and get involved in ongoing research projects. This gives the interns a wide perspective of this sector, allowing them to decide whether to continue pursuing a career in this sector or not. Internship opportunities are given to students who have degrees in, but not limited to:

  • Medicine
  • Medical Microbiology
  • Microbiology and Biotechnology
  • Mycology
  • Virology
  • Biochemistry
  • Analytical Chemistry
  • Parasitology
  • Molecular/Biomedical Sciences
  • Pharmaceutical Sciences
  • Public Health
  • Health Systems
  • Biostatistics
  • Social Science

Those with diplomas in are also eligible for the internship in the following fields:

  • Health Records
  • Science Laboratory technology

Apart from the technical internship opportunities, the institute offers internship opportunities in the support services institute. This comprises bachelor holders in:

  • Accounting
  • Procurement and Supply
  • Human Resource Management
  • Information Technology Communication
  • Mass Communication in Electronic Media
  • Law (must have completed pupilage/awaiting admission to the bar)
  • Economics

Diploma holders are also considered for these support internship opportunities. Those with diplomas in the following areas are considered:

  • Mechanical Engineering
  • Records Management
  • Graphic Design

KEMRI releases a list highlighting the internship opportunities available and the number of individuals needed. The application process usually involves filling out the KEMRI internship application form.

How to apply for KEMRI internships

The KEMRI internship application in 2024 is quite simple but follows a set of rules. For starters, one has to meet all the eligibility criteria for him or her to be considered for the internship. Some of these eligibility criteria include:

  • The applicant has to write a formal letter to the Director of KEMRI.
  • At the time of application, the applicant has to be in a learning facility or institution for more than 12 months or not have completed his/her education in more than two years ago.
  • The internship programme will only be done once; thus, make it count.
  • The internship period will not exceed six months and won't be renewed at the time of expiry.
  • Those who have retired and lost their job do not qualify for the internship programme.

Besides these, the applicant has to have some personal attributes that meet the institute's expectations. These include:

  • The applicant must have interpersonal skills and good communication skills.
  • He/she has to be goal-driven, a self-starter, flexible, and passionate.
  • He or she should uphold integrity at all times.
  • The applicant has to have strong analytical skills.
  • He/she should be well-versed in computers.

These should be accompanied by a certificate of good conduct from the relevant authority. The applicant must be a Kenyan youth aged 30 years or less.

KEMRI internship selection process

KEMRI recruits applicants through an extensive process, which selects only a few qualified applicants. The HR Department determines the internship vacancies in response to requests from Center Directors and Department heads.

An advertisement is then posted on the website and/or local newspapers. Applications are reviewed based on eligibility criteria and the availability of internship fields.

Selection is conducted through a competitive process emphasizing merit, diversity in subject areas, gender balance, regional representation, and inclusion of people with disabilities. Successful candidates receive notification and a formal letter of offer.

Does KEMRI pay interns?

KEMRI offers a monthly stipend to interns. KEMRI internship salary varies depending on your qualification level. The stipend is Ksh. 25,000 for bachelor's degree holders and Ksh. 15,000 for diploma holders.

What qualifications do you need for KEMRI?

The specific qualifications for a KEMRI internship application depend on the advertised position. Generally, applicants should possess a bachelor's degree or diploma certificate. They should also not be in any learning institution at the time of application.

Does KEMRI offer attachment?

The institute offers an attachment program that runs quarterly, with intakes in January, April, July, and October.

How does KEMRI work?

KEMRI functions through a network of regional and specialized research centres across Kenya. Each centre focuses on specific areas of public health concern and offers diverse internship opportunities.

KEMRI provides internship vacancies in Kenya, which is in line with the Kenyan government's Vision 2030. This is in a bid to increase the employability of Kenyan youths by creating graduates who are exposed to a practical working environment.
