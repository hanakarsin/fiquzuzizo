Queen Elizabeth II knights war veteran Tom Moore after raising KSh 4.4 billion

April 2024 · 3 minute read

- One former World War II soldier proved that you can never be too old to do anything and he got rewarded for it

- Tom Moore pledged to walk 100 laps around his garden to raise funds for healthcare workers

- Moore ended up raising KSh 4.4 billion and was knighted by Queen Elizabeth II for his efforts

Queen Elizabeth II knighted 100-year-old World War II veteran Captain Tom Moore, who became a national celebrity by raising millions for the United Kingdom's National Health Service (NHS).

The now Sir Tom Moore was knighted in an outdoor ceremony at Windsor Castle on Friday, July 17.

Moore pledged to walk 100 laps around his garden to raise funds for healthcare workers.

He raised almost KSh 4.4 billion ( £32 million) by walking laps of his backyard, after initially planning to raise only KSh 140,000.

In her first official engagement in person since the lockdown, Queen Elizabeth II emerged in the grounds of Windsor Castle for the unique ceremony.

Her arrival was announced by the sound of bagpipes played by the Queen’s Piper, Pipe Major Richard Grisdale of the Royal Regiment of Scotland.

She knighted Moore with her father's sword and praised him for his fundraising efforts, saying, according to the PA Media news agency:

"Thank you so much, an amazing amount of money you raised."

Moore, who rose to the rank of captain during his military career, was recently promoted to honorary colonel in recognition of his efforts.

Downing Street confirmed Captain Tom's knighthood was approved by the Queen on Tuesday, May 19, after UK Prime Minister Boris Johnson put in a special nomination for the 100-year-old.

Moore had earlier spoken to Good Morning Britain after his knighthood announcement and expressed his surprise and gratitude.

"I'm looking forward to that. I hope she's not very heavy-handed with the sword because by then I might be rather a poor old weak soul," Moore quipped.

"I am absolutely thrilled Her Majesty has decided – or chosen poor little me. It really is a great honour and something I never, ever anticipated," he modestly said.

Adding he was overwhelmed by the support and affection he received, Moore thanked those who donated to his course and the officials who made his knighthood a possibility.
