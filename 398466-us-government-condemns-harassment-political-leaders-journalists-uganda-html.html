US government condemns harassment of political leaders, journalists in Uganda

May 2024 · 3 minute read

- US slammed Ugandan authorities for what it termed as excessive use of force while enforcing law and order during political rallies

- According to US Mission Uganda, seizure of politicians, group detentions and harassment of journalists showed total disregard of democracy by Ugandan authorities

- Supporters of firebrand politician Bobi Wine have been on the receiving end of police force during the ongoing campaigns ahead of the General Election expected on January 14

The United States has strongly condemned the "disproportionate deployment of force, seizure of a political party leader, group detentions, and harassment of journalists" in Uganda.

In a statement, the US Mission Uganda described the current developments in the Ugandan political landscape as outcomes of an administration that was not keen on entrenching democracy and protecting lives.

Tension has been mounting in Uganda as the country heads to the General Election slated for January 14, 2021.

Dozens of citizens have been killed and scores injured in clashes between police and supporters of Kyadondo East MP and presidential candidate Bobi Wine.

Among victims of the violent clashes are journalists with various media houses, and the police have been accused of using excessive force.

On Wednesday, December 30, a Nation Media Group journalist was arrested while covering Bobi Wine's campaigns.

Security forces have been accused of being used to shift the political landscape in favour of President Yoweri Museveni who is eying to recapture the seat he has held since 1986.

"The disproportionate use of force, the capture of a political party leader, group detentions, and harassment of reporters are not the sign of security forces who care about democracy and the future of the Ugandan people," US Mission Uganda said Wednesday.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Bobi Wine was seized by police on Wednesday, December 30, when he took his campaign trail to Kalangala area.

According to the politician, he was bundled in a prison van where he was teargassed, then later flown to his home aboard an army helicopter.

Some of his campaign team members are still in detention.

Uganda Police Force (UPF) denied arresting the lawmaker stating that they only prevented him from holding large political rallies to stop the spread of COVID-19 as directed by the Ministry of Health and the country's poll agency.

To ensure journalists covering political rallies were protected, UPF said it had rolled out an accreditation programme stating that only registered reporters will be allowed to cover such events.

"We would like to further remind all accredited journalists that commanders have been given a list of all successfully accredited journalists for ease of verification. This arrangement will help us accord journalists better protection.Journalists subjected to any form of physical abuse and other forms of harassment in the course of duty are reminded to report their cases to the media crimes department at CID headquarters, " a police statement issued Wednesday read.
